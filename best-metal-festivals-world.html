Festival Guide

The Best Metal Festivals
in the World

OneHeavy Josef Den Dunnen April 2026 7 min read

For heavy metal fans, festivals are the pilgrimage. The chance to see dozens of favourite bands across several days, surrounded by tens of thousands of like-minded people, is an experience unlike anything else in music. These are the world's greatest heavy metal festivals — the events that every serious metal fan should experience at least once.

Wacken Open Air — Germany's Metal Cathedral

Wacken Open Air is the undisputed king of heavy metal festivals. Held annually in the tiny village of Wacken in northern Germany since 1990, it has grown from a 800-person local event into the world's largest metal festival, welcoming over 85,000 fans each year. The festival site transforms the surrounding farmland into a temporary metal city, complete with multiple stages, camping for tens of thousands, and a lineup that consistently features the biggest names in heavy metal alongside emerging acts from around the world.

The atmosphere at Wacken is legendary — a genuine sense of community and shared passion that is unlike any other festival in the world. The audience is knowledgeable, passionate and deeply respectful of the music and the artists. Wacken has hosted virtually every major metal act in history at some point, from Metallica and Iron Maiden to Slayer, Judas Priest and AC/DC.

Wacken Open Air is not just a festival. It is the spiritual home of heavy metal — a place where the global metal community gathers to celebrate the music that defines their lives.

Download Festival — The UK's Premier Metal Event

Held annually at Donington Park in Leicestershire, Download Festival has been the centrepiece of the UK heavy music calendar since 2003. The festival site at Donington Park has a legendary history in rock and metal stretching back to the Monsters of Rock events of the 1980s, which makes Download feel like part of a continuous tradition rather than a modern commercial event.

Download typically features three days of heavy music across multiple stages, with a lineup that spans classic heavy metal through to modern metalcore and everything in between. The festival has hosted landmark performances from Metallica, AC/DC, Guns N' Roses, Linkin Park and countless others.

Hellfest — France's Heavy Music Jewel

Hellfest in Clisson, western France, has established itself as one of Europe's most beloved and respected metal festivals since its founding in 2006. The festival is known for its extraordinarily diverse lineup — spanning every corner of heavy music from classic rock to extreme metal — and for the genuine passion and organisation that goes into its staging.

Hellfest regularly attracts over 60,000 fans per day and has become a pilgrimage destination for European metal fans. The festival's commitment to celebrating the full breadth of heavy music, from vintage NWOBHM acts to cutting-edge extreme metal, makes it genuinely unique among European festivals.

Soundwave — Australia's Lost Festival Giant

No guide to metal festivals would be complete without acknowledging Soundwave — the Australian touring festival that ran from 2004 to 2015 and represented a golden era for heavy music in Australia. Soundwave brought the biggest names in rock and metal to Australian audiences at a time when such visits were rare, and its lineups were genuinely world class, featuring Metallica, Slipknot, Linkin Park, System of a Down and dozens of others.

The loss of Soundwave in 2015 left a significant gap in the Australian live music landscape that has only partially been filled. Its legacy lives on in the thousands of Australian metal fans who experienced their first major international metal events through its stages.

Metal for the Masses — Smaller Festivals Worth Your Time

Beyond the giants, the global metal festival circuit includes dozens of smaller but equally passionate events. Maryland Deathfest in Baltimore is the world's premier extreme metal festival, celebrating underground death metal, grindcore and related genres with a ferocity and knowledge that is unmatched. Brutal Assault in the Czech Republic offers one of the most adventurous and eclectic extreme metal lineups in Europe. Bloodstock Open Air in the UK provides a more intimate alternative to Download with a focus on underground and emerging metal acts.

For Australian fans, the absence of a regular dedicated metal festival remains a genuine gap in the local music landscape.
