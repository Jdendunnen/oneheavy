Genre Guide

Industrial Metal

The most mechanised branch of heavy metal. Industrial Metal fuses heavy guitar riffs with electronic beats, synthesisers and the cold, mechanical aesthetic of industrial music.

History & Overview

Origins — Ministry and Nine Inch Nails

Industrial metal emerged in the late 1980s and early 1990s from the intersection of heavy metal and industrial music. American band Ministry, under the leadership of Al Jourgensen, were instrumental in developing the genre, blending heavy guitar riffs with programmed beats, samples and the abrasive aesthetic of industrial music. Nine Inch Nails, while not strictly a metal band, brought industrial music to a massive mainstream audience and influenced countless industrial metal acts with their dense, layered approach to heavy music.

Rammstein and European Industrial Metal

German band Rammstein became the most commercially successful industrial metal band in history. Their combination of grinding guitar riffs, electronic elements, theatrical performances and German language lyrics created a unique and immediately identifiable sound that connected with audiences worldwide. Rammstein's 2001 album Mutter is widely considered the definitive industrial metal recording and remains one of the best-selling metal albums of the modern era.

Fear Factory and Mechanical Metal

American band Fear Factory developed a distinctive brand of industrial metal that emphasised the mechanical and technological aspects of the genre. Their 1995 album Demanufacture is considered one of the finest industrial metal recordings ever made, featuring extraordinarily precise drumming, downtuned guitar riffs and the contrast between clean and harsh vocals. Fear Factory's influence on modern heavy metal, particularly on djent and progressive metal, is enormous.

Industrial Metal Today

Industrial metal today encompasses a wide range of sounds from the mainstream accessibility of Rammstein and Five Finger Death Punch to the experimental extremity of Portal and Godflesh. The genre's fusion of human aggression and mechanical precision remains one of the most distinctive and compelling sounds in all of heavy music.

Key Bands

Rammstein, Fear Factory, Nine Inch Nails, Ministry, Strapping Young Lad, Front Line Assembly, Godkiller, Sybreed, KMFDM, White Zombie
