Band Profile

Hidden Intent
Adelaide's Thrash Champions

OneHeavy Josef Den Dunnen April 2026 5 min read

Hidden Intent are the pride of the Adelaide heavy metal scene and one of the most respected underground thrash metal acts working anywhere in the world today. The South Australian trio have spent over fifteen years building an international reputation for uncompromising old-school thrash metal, relentless touring and genuine commitment to the traditional values of heavy music. This is their story.

The Adelaide Origins

Hidden Intent formed in Adelaide in 2007 around the Barnard brothers — vocalist and bassist Chris Barnard and drummer Tom Barnard — alongside guitarist Mitch Conaghty. The trio set out with a clear vision: to play uncompromising old-school thrash metal in the tradition of Slayer, Kreator, Exodus and the classic Bay Area thrash acts of the 1980s.

The band emerged from the small but fiercely dedicated Adelaide heavy metal scene — a scene that has historically been overshadowed by the larger metal communities in Melbourne, Sydney and Brisbane but that has consistently produced bands of genuine quality and international significance. Hidden Intent quickly established themselves as one of the most impressive young thrash metal bands in Australia through relentless local touring and a clear commitment to the traditional thrash metal aesthetic.

International Recognition

Hidden Intent's second album Liberation from Dominion (2013) was the record that brought the band to international attention. The album's combination of technically impressive thrash metal songwriting, ferocious vocals and a production aesthetic that deliberately evoked the classic thrash recordings of the 1980s earned widespread critical acclaim from thrash metal publications worldwide.

Hidden Intent are among the most respected underground thrash metal bands working anywhere in the world. They are Adelaide's most important contribution to international heavy music.

The album opened doors to international touring opportunities that would transform the band's trajectory. Hidden Intent have since toured extensively across Europe, Asia and North America, sharing stages with many of the classic thrash metal acts who inspired them and establishing themselves as genuine international contenders.

The Execution

The 2017 album The Execution represented a significant artistic step forward. The record demonstrated that Hidden Intent were not simply traditional thrash revivalists but were capable of writing genuinely great thrash metal songs that could stand alongside the classic works of the genre. The Execution received extraordinary critical acclaim and cemented the band's international reputation.

The band have continued to tour relentlessly internationally, making them among the most dedicated touring thrash metal acts working today. Their commitment to the DIY values of the underground heavy metal scene, their uncompromising approach to heaviness and their willingness to tour in challenging conditions have made them genuinely respected across the global thrash metal community.

Why Hidden Intent Matter

Hidden Intent are Adelaide's most important contribution to international heavy metal. They are the band that has most clearly demonstrated that Adelaide can produce world-class heavy metal acts despite the city's smaller scene and historic disadvantages relative to the larger Australian capitals. Their international touring success, their critical acclaim within the thrash metal community and their uncompromising commitment to traditional heavy metal values have made them ambassadors for Adelaide metal worldwide.

For South Australian metal fans in particular, Hidden Intent represent genuine pride. They are proof that Adelaide metal can compete at the absolute highest international level of underground heavy metal. The Barnard brothers and Conaghty have built something genuinely remarkable from a city that many would have considered too small and too isolated to support an internationally successful heavy metal act.
